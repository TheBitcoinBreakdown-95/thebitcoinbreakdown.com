**@ericyakes** (Eric Yakes)

Custodial systems exist due to market demand, will only continue to, and will likely expand as bitcoin institutionalizes. 

Ecash has the potential to optimize these systems and will play a key role in reducing barriers to entry for custodial operations. Lowering the marginal cost to spin up a bank and afford people trusted payment solutions will have systemic impacts on the distributed nature of custodial operations that I don’t yet think we can understand. 

Trustless systems will have an incremental cost over trusted and until that changes market actors will demand them. Barring a zero to one innovation I think trustless will simply be optimizing around the current state but never becoming economically cheaper than trusted.

Both systems will be used in tandem for a digitally native system to emerge and what’s important is that it is systemically distributed enough to remove any chance of political capture. Imagine ecash banks in more localized functions with LN nodes acting as clearing houses for trustless settlement. None of this is either or and different tools will be used for different functions.
