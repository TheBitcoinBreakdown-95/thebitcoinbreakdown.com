## PGP: Whats all this about then?

Email and other electronic forms of communication are much more vulnerable to snooping, government or otherwise, than conventional forms of communication. Phil Zimmermann wrote PGP to enable ordinary people to protect their electronic communications from prying eyes using strong encryption. PGP combines public key cryptography (based on the RSA algorithm) with conventional cryptography (using the IDEA algorithm) to give fast encryption without the need for a prior exchange of secret keys. PGP can also be used to authenticate the originator of a message. Here is what Phil Zimmermann has to say about [PGP and why you might want to use it.](pgp.html)

### [How PGP and RSA works](howpgp.html)

Here is a link to a document explaining **[how PGP and RSA works](howpgp.html)**. It explains the maths behind RSA and includes a brief outline of modular arithmetic and some of the mathmatical ideas used in RSA. The maths isn't too fierce but it helps if you know what prime numbers are. 

### The creator of PGP under investigation

Phil Zimmermann was being investigated by the US government for "exporting" his cryptographic software, which is classed as a munition under ITAR regulations. The investigation has finally been dropped...

Message from Phil, the creator of PGP, to the cypherpunks list...

**My lead defense lawyer, Phil Dubois, received a fax this morning from the Assistant US Attorney in Northern District of California, William Keane. The letter informed us that I "will not be prosecuted in connection with the posting to USENET in June 1991 of the encryption program Pretty Good Privacy. The investigation is closed."**

[Read the rest of the message](phreephil.html)

### PGP & privacy issues

All kinds of people, worried about invasion of privacy on computer networks, are using and propogating PGP all over the world. A number of [organisations and groups](privacy.html) have also sprung up dedicated to protecting the rights of individuals on the internet. 

For more information about privacy issues, cryptography & PGP, you might want to try these WWW pages: 

  * [Cryptography, PGP, and Your Privacy](http://world.std.com/~franl/crypto.html), a very comprehensive source of technical and other information from the WWW virtual library maintained by Francis Litterio. **Web page maintainers note - This site has changed URL: http://world.std.com/~franl/crypto.html.**
  * [Marjorie Wisby's Australian "Free Speech Under Attack" Page](http://www.ultra.net.au/~wisby/alert.htm)
  * [The cryptography FAQ](http://www.cis.ohio-state.edu/hypertext/faq/usenet/cryptography-faq/top.html) which comes in 10 parts is an excellent starting point for anyone interested in cryptography. 
  * [The UKERNA Project](http://tech.ukerna.ac.uk/pgp/ukerna.html) to make PGP a security standard for the UK's academic community 
  * [Nat Queen's "Introduction to PGP" page](http://sun1.bham.ac.uk/N.M.Queen/pgp/pgp.html)
  * [Ralph Craig has links to remailer info on this page - check out the WWW remailer interface](http://www4.ncsu.edu/eos/users/r/rrcraig/www/crypto.html)
  * [Mathew's PGP home page in the UK](http://www.mantis.co.uk/pgp/pgp.html) \- a good place for info about PGP2.6ui and links to versions of PGP suitable for most platforms 
  * ['Crypto Rebels' a WIRED article](http://www.wired.com/Etext/1.2/features/crypto.rebels.html) about the development of public key cryptography, PGP, and the cypherpunks. Logon to Wired as "cypherpunks", password "cypherpunks". 
  * [The Cypherpunks home page](ftp://scotch.csua.berkeley.edu/pub/cypherpunks/Home.html) with lots of good technical info and useful pointers 
  * [Mukund Mohan's Cryptography Page ](http://retriever.cs.umbc.edu/~mohan/Work/crypt.html) with a comprehensive index of PGP/privacy WWW pages.

## How to get PGP - Version 2.6.3i now available!

The latest international version of PGP is now available here for most platforms. This is an Australian site for quicker downloads down-under. If you live elsewhere then use a site nearer you. For info about other sites and for more detailed information about PGP 2.6.3 check out the [The International PGP Home Page](http://www.ifi.uio.no/pgp)

_If you live in the US, you should download PGP from[MIT](http://web.mit.edu/network/pgp.html) instead._

**[Download the latest international version of PGP via WWW here](PGP/download.html). The same files are also locally available by [FTP](ftp://rschp1.anu.edu.au/pub/pgp/).**

Supported Platforms include: UNIX, DOS, 32-bit DOS, Windows, Mac, Powermac, Atari & Amiga. 

For Acorn RISC OS (Archimedes) users, an older version of PGP is available here: [pgp262i.arc](ftp://ftp.ox.ac.uk/pub/crypto/pgp/archimedes/pgp262i.arc) _non-local link - letme know of any problems
