ET Spotlight Special

A large percentage of women believe that financial independence is of utmost importance to them. Yet if we look around the cryptocurrency space, women comprise a small percentage of the crypto space. For example, [CoinSwitch Kuber](https://bit.ly/2SFuRr5) reports that women form merely 15% of their user base.  
  
**Why do women stay away from crypto?  
** There are many speculations as to why cryptocurrency remains predominantly male.   


  


  1. Cryptocurrency is generally looked at from two angles - tech and finance. Women in technology and finance are often overlooked and underrepresented. Some also believe that these two fields are predominantly male.
  2. Another theory is that women are risk-averse when it comes to investments. Since cryptocurrency is known for volatility, women tend to stick to traditional investments.

However, that's not just it. A study reveals that more female investors (76%) than male investors (52%) confessed that they lack the familiarity of this new asset class. Women do not invest because they are not exposed to the asset class. On the other hand, women are interested in financial products that make sense to them.

**Not just a 'Boys club' anymore  
** The year 2020 was phenomenal for the crypto space in India. After the Supreme Court clarified the legality of the asset class, it saw a steady rise in women investors entering the field. The cryptocurrency platform CoinSwitch Kuber witnessed a whopping 1000% increase in its number of women user registrations in the past year.   


### Live Events


  


Across the globe, more women are seen to show interest in this new asset class. According to a study conducted by Grayscale, women are more willing to make investment decisions based on the education provided in the asset class. Interestingly, 93% of women have stated that they would be more open to investing in cryptocurrency if they were better educated on the subject.  


ET Spotlight Special

Contrary to the popular sentiment, cryptocurrency isn't just for men. Let's take Uma, a CoinSwitch Kuber user; she states that her first-ever investment experience started with cryptocurrencies. Another crypto investor Chaitra claims that cryptocurrency investments helped her pay off her education loans. These testimonials stand as proof that more modern women are open to exploring cryptocurrencies nowadays.  


  
While it’s true that women still form a small percentage of the crypto community, women have been putting up a tough fight in the Indian market.   


  
Apart from the rise in the number of female investors, the overall percentage of women working in the Indian crypto space is increasing at a quicker pace. According to Swati Pincha, the growth lead at CoinSwitch Kuber, “Crypto industry in India is going through some very exciting times, and it's exciting to have the ownership of building it responsibly. It’s good to see many women working in a space that was traditionally believed to be male-dominated. Having a good mix of both men and women in the organization has helped us truly make crypto investing easy.”   
  
**Why are women the better crypto investors?  
** Two distinct factors make women better crypto investors.  
  
Firstly, women are savers - A study by Fidelity Investments revealed that women hold about 8.3% of their income into savings (men save only 7.9% of their income). More savings means that women have more power to diversify their investments into high-return assets like cryptocurrencies.   
  
Secondly, women are more successful investors - Women tend to evaluate risks in almost everything and mitigate them in the best way possible. According to a Merrill Lynch research female investors tend to weigh risks before investing in an asset class. Hence, female investors can be more analytical with their investments. While investing in a volatile market such as crypto, their cautiousness nudges them into making the right decision.   
  
**Can crypto be the way forward for financial inclusion?  
** The Global Gender Gap Index, a report on the overall disparity between men and women across 149 countries by the World Economic Forum, found that in nearly 40% of the countries, women do not have equal access to financial services. Without the ability to open bank accounts and transact money, women lose opportunities to grow and succeed.  
  
India is moving forward towards better financial inclusion. Yet, some parts of rural India still witness low participation of women in economic activities. Due to the lack of financial awareness and facilities available for these women to be financially independent.   
  
Cryptocurrencies can be very liberating for women, especially those who do not have access to a bank account. Furthermore, since cryptocurrency has low barriers to entry, you only need a smartphone with an internet connection to get started. Considering that it does not require anyone to seek approval from a third party, crypto can be a powerful tool to help the world move towards financial inclusion and closing the gender gap.  
  
**How do crypto platforms contribute towards financial inclusion?  
** Although cryptocurrency platforms are seeing more women investors onboard their platforms each day, women remain underrepresented in the crypto community. Platforms like [CoinSwitch Kuber](https://coinswitch.co/) are striving to make crypto accessible to all regardless of gender.   
  
**They aim to close the gender gap in the community by offering:  
**

  * Free Educational Resources such as [Kuberverse](https://bit.ly/3gFXpIO) as an attempt to educate people about money and cryptos.
  * A simplified approach to crypto investing for non-tech savvy investors.
  * Fractional investment allows anyone with just ₹100 to be able to invest in cryptocurrencies.

There is still a lot of work to be done for better involvement of women in crypto. Thankfully, there is an ever-growing community of crypto enthusiasts, organizations, platforms and forums committed to help others understand cryptocurrencies. These steps could be the foundation for better financial inclusion in the crypto world.
