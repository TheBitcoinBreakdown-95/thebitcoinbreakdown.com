# Where to Go From Here

Let's take a step back and look at what you've built.

You've got VS Code on your computer with Claude Code inside it. You've got a project folder with real files in it... files you directed an AI agent to create. You've got a CLAUDE.md file that teaches the agent who you are and how you work. You've used your Agent Control Center to learn, organize, plan, and execute.

And everything... every file, every plan, every document... lives on your computer. In folders you control. In plain text you can open with anything.

That's not a chatbot. That's a new way of using a computer.

---

## What You've Actually Learned

This course didn't teach you to code. It didn't teach you "prompt engineering." It didn't teach you a bunch of tricks that'll be obsolete in six months.

It taught you something simpler and more durable than any of that.

You learned that AI works best when you direct it from your own machine, not when you chat with it in a browser. You learned that files on your computer are more valuable than conversations in someone else's platform. You learned that the environment you build around AI matters more than the questions you ask it. And you learned that AI is a partner, not a replacement... and that staying in the driver's seat is what makes the whole thing work.

These aren't tricks. These are principles. They'll apply to whatever AI looks like next year, and the year after that.

---

## What's Next

There's a lot more you can do with this setup. A lot more.

Your Agent Control Center has capabilities we didn't even touch. Memory systems that let the agent remember things across sessions without you having to put everything in CLAUDE.md. Slash commands that let you trigger complex workflows with a single word. Rules files that enforce standards automatically. Hooks that run before or after the agent does something. Parallel agents that can work on multiple things at the same time.

That's the follow-up course... Intro to Claude Code. It goes deep on the tools and workflows that turn a good Agent Control Center into a great one. Think of this course as learning to drive. The follow-up course is learning everything the car can actually do.

But you don't need any of that to start getting value right now. You already have everything you need. A workspace. An agent. Your files. Your direction.

---

## The Bigger Picture

You just learned a new way to interact with a computer. Not clicking buttons. Not navigating menus. Not memorizing keyboard shortcuts. Talking. Directing. Describing what you want and watching it happen.

This is where computing is going. Not for programmers. Not for tech people. For everyone. The ability to direct AI agents in plain English is going to be as fundamental as knowing how to use a search engine. It already is, most people just don't know it yet.

And you do.
