[How to Start a Podcast 2020: Podcasting for Beginners](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NUmdoFDtpQg)
 - Three types of mics
    - USB - digital
    - Dynamic and Condenser mics which are analog
- USB mic should be the easiest
    - Just plug and go
    - Look for a built in headphone jack and volume control on the usb mic so you can adjust your levels in real time
- Front vs side address design
    - The side address will pick up sounds from the room
    - Front address mic has a precise pickup range for good rejection of background noise
- IF your podcast has two or more people you SHOULD NOT use USB mics
    - Dynamic mics help reject background noise, and are good when more than one person is speaking, BUT you have to keep your face at the same distance to avoid changing volume levels
    - Condenser mics need phantom power or plus 48v, gives them enough juice to operate properly, they are also more sensitive than dynamics so they're better for quiet or professional recording environments
        - You can use dynamic or condenser mics to record into a separate audio recorder like Zoom H6, good for portability
        - Portable recorders tend to have less professional preamps and interfaces, and they often only have one headphone output so you may need to use an external headphone amp if you have multiple people in your podcast
- Standalone podcast machine
    - Can record to the device alone, and use it as an interface to your computer for recording to software
    - Multiple mic inputs, faders to control audio levels, sound pads for audio effects, multiple headphone outputs for podcast talent etc.
- Headphones
    - He says to invest in good studio headphones
        - You might need a headphone amp if you have more than one person
- Pop filter is the round thing I like to play with, helps with p's t's and c's
- Do I still have the boom arm?
- If you are calling people into your show, try to get gear that has a mixed minus function which enables you to integrate phone calls into your show clearly
- Audio editing software
    - Audacity, garage band and pro tools
- Editing
    - Normalize audio
    - Eq to remove harsh sounds
    - Compressor to tame loud spikes
    - Use a limiter to adhere to loudness standards
- Podcast hosting
    - Where all of your episodes will live
    - Popular sites are
        - Buzzsprout
        - Captivate
        - Podbean
        - Simplecast
        - Etc
    - For every episode you create, the host will provide a syndicated feed to platforms that actually play your podcasts such as itunes stitcher, spotify, soundcloud

To get rid of background static:

- Select 5 seconds of silent audio - effect - noise reduction
- First click get noise profile
    - Set noise reduction to 12
    - Sensitivity to 12.5
    - Frequency smoothing bands 7
    - Click the button that says reduce
- Then select all audio
