**🧠** **I. Foundations: Understand How People Learn**  
**1. Cognitive Load Theory (Sweller)**

- **Principle**: Don’t overload working memory. People can only handle a few pieces of new info at once.
- **Strategy**: Break content into manageable "chunks" and sequence from simple to complex. Use visuals and analogies to reduce mental strain.

**2. Constructivism (Piaget, Vygotsky)**

- **Principle**: People learn by connecting new info to what they already know.
- **Strategy**: Always start by asking, _“What do you already know or believe about this?”_ Then, build from there with stories, real-world examples, or thought experiments.

**3. Social Learning Theory (Bandura)**

- **Principle**: We learn by observing others and modeling behavior.
- **Strategy**: Demonstrate thinking aloud. Model curiosity. Show mistakes. Let learners _see_ your process.
 
**🔁** **II. Absorption: Make Content Stick**  
**1. The ELI5 Rule (Explain Like I'm 5)**

- You must be able to distill an idea so clearly that a child could understand. This forces clarity in both you and your learner.

**2. Active Retrieval & Spaced Repetition**

- Don’t just _re-expose_ learners to information — test them on it. Recalling info is how we encode it.
- Review material in spaced intervals: Day 1, Day 2, Day 4, Day 7, etc.

**3. Dual Coding Theory**

- Combine **words + visuals** to activate different memory systems.
- Teach with diagrams, timelines, sketches, infographics, or metaphors.

**4. Socratic Interrogation**

- Don’t give the answer. Ask just the right question to lead them to it.
- Example: _“What do you think would happen if…?”_ or _“How could we test that?”_
 
**🔥** **III. Inspiration: Cultivate Curiosity and Intrinsic Motivation**  
**1. Make It a Mystery**

- Begin a lesson with a provocative question, problem, or contradiction.
- Example: _“Why do zebras have stripes — and what does that have to do with data privacy?”_

**2. Let Them Struggle… Briefly**

- “Desirable difficulty” boosts learning. Don’t rescue too soon. Let them wrestle with confusion for a moment — this primes the brain to retain the answer better.

**3. Give Autonomy + Challenge + Purpose (Self-Determination Theory)**

- Let them choose part of the process: what problem to solve, what example to explore.
- Give challenges that match their level. And connect the topic to a bigger meaning.
 
**🎭** **IV. Delivery: How You Teach Matters**  
**1. Tell Stories**

- Humans are wired for narrative. Even abstract ideas stick better when wrapped in a story or personal anecdote.
- Good stories have tension, emotion, and a moral or insight.

**2. Use the Feynman Technique**

- Teach a concept back in simple terms, then identify gaps and refine. Let your learner do the same — it’s the ultimate test of understanding.

**3. Engage the Body**

- Get learners to draw it, act it out, build a model, or even walk it through physically.
- Kinesthetic techniques are underused but powerful.
 
**🧪** **V. Practice: Let Them Teach It**  
You don’t really _own_ an idea until you can teach it. Give your learner a chance to:

- Explain a concept to others
- Create a video or infographic
- Write a “teach-back” summary
- Debate it from both sides
 
**🛠** **VI. Tool Kit: Techniques to Use Repeatedly**

|   |   |   |
|---|---|---|
|**Technique**|**What It Does**|**Example**|
|**Think-Pair-Share**|Sparks dialogue|Pose a question, have learners think solo, then discuss with a peer|
|**Concept Maps**|Connect ideas|Let learners draw webs of how ideas interrelate|
|**Exit Tickets**|Reflection|“What’s one question you still have?” or “What was your biggest takeaway?”|
|**Analogies**|Make abstract ideas relatable|“This is like a traffic jam in your brain”|
|**Chunk & Zoom**|Control scope|Teach the overview first, then zoom into details|
   

**🧭** **VII. Final Golden Rule: Teach with Enthusiasm and Openness**  
Your energy _transmits_. If you model curiosity, humility, and joy in learning — it gives learners permission to do the same. And if you _ask_ questions instead of delivering pronouncements, you shift the culture from passive absorption to active exploration.
