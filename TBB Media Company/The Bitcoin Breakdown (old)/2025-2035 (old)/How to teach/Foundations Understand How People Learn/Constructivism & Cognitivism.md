A teacher who uses videos, stories, and stresses application of knowledge instead of rote memorization falls into which style
 
**ChatGPT said:**  
A teacher who emphasizes **videos, storytelling, and real-world application** over rote memorization is primarily aligned with **constructivist** and **cognitivist** learning theories, often drawing on aspects of **experiential** and **social learning** as well.  
Here’s a breakdown of how these styles map onto that teaching approach:
 
**✅** **Primary Style: Constructivism**

- **Why?**
    - Focuses on learners _constructing_ their own understanding.
    - Uses stories and real-world examples to **build on prior knowledge**.
    - Stresses **authentic tasks**, critical thinking, and open-ended exploration over recall.

**Constructivist teachers** guide students to discover meaning, not memorize facts.
 
**✅** **Complementary Style: Cognitivism**

- **Why?**
    - Uses structured inputs (like videos and stories) to support **mental processing and schema formation**.
    - Stresses **application**, encoding information deeply by connecting it to relevant contexts.
    - Supports chunking and retrieval — e.g., stories help memory.

**Cognitivist teachers** scaffold knowledge to help students absorb, store, and retrieve it effectively.
 
**✅** **Also Integrated: Experiential Learning (Kolb)**

- **Why?**
    - Application of knowledge is the core of experiential learning.
    - Learners **do something**, then reflect and apply insight.
    - Videos and stories offer rich simulated experiences.

Think: "Learn by doing, reflecting, adapting."
 
**✅** **Possible Add-On: Social Learning (Bandura)**

- If the teacher uses videos of **others modeling behavior**, or has students learn through observing peers — this overlaps with **social learning theory**.
 
**❌** **Not a Fit: Behaviorism**

- Behaviorist teachers rely on **repetition, rewards, and drills** — i.e., rote memorization.
- This teacher is actively rejecting that paradigm.
 
**Summary:**  
**Teaching Style → Constructivist–Cognitivist Hybrid**

- _Tools_: Videos, stories, real-world contexts
- _Method_: Conceptual understanding, not memorization
- _Goal_: Deep learning, personal meaning, transferability
