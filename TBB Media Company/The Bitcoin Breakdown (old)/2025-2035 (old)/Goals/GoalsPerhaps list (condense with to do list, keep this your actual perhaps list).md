**Meditation**:  
My meditation goal is to set some time aside each day to give my mind a break. Not counting wim hof or mindfulness practices, I will meditate for a minimum of 10 mins a day. I would like to use a guided meditation every day in the beginning as I'm developing my habit and then as I start to do it on my own I won't have to. Measuring my success is just the consistency, but I will not judge myself if I miss a day. I will not count meditating high, since it's not real meditation. I would like to incorporate into my morning routine, but its ok if it comes later, I just have to make it a point. There is no end to this goal, or knowing when it is complete. When I find my day incomplete without meditation, like how I feel I can't start my day without a shower, then I will know I have made it.  
Specific:  
What do I want to accomplish? Both getting into the habit of meditating, as well as meditating every day because I enjoy it.  
Why is this goal important? Meditation brings patience, it brings mindfulness, it brings awareness of my own mind, and it reduces stress. Self-control and emotional awareness are things I desperately need, and meditation can help.  
Who is involved? Just me.  
Where is it located? Wherever whenever, but I would like to meditate in the morning or little times when I need to.  
Which resources or limits are involved? My time and energy. Not falling asleep during, and making time for the practice are the only constraints.  
Measurable  
How much? Not counting breathing meditation, 20-30 mins a day. 10 minute guided can count towards my time.  
How many? Once per day, but whenever I feel like it maybe in between meetings or at night to calm my mind.  
How will I know when it is accomplished? When I create the habit and don't skip any days, when I do it without thinking because I want to.  
Achievable  
How can I accomplish this goal? Being determined and having self-control.  
How realistic is the goal, based on other constraints, such as financial factors? Very realistic, I have time for this I just need to learn to sacrifice time on my phone which I don't actually enjoy, I'm just addicted.
