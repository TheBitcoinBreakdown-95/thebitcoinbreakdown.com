## RULE 1: Not Your Keys, Not Your Bitcoin (3 min)

**[TALKING POINTS -- riff from these]**

- The most important rule. The one that separates bitcoiners from crypto tourists.

- For those that don't know... in 2014, the centralized exchange Mt. Gox -- which was basically the only Bitcoin exchange around back then -- was hacked. 850,000 bitcoin stolen. About 4% of the entire supply. Gone.

- It was a massive moment in the history of Bitcoin. Many people didn't think it would survive afterwards. The price went from over $1,000 to under $200.

- And ever since then, it was part of Bitcoin culture that you don't trust centralized custodians with your money. In fact, removing trust from financial institutions was the whole point that Bitcoin was even created.

- Since then we've experienced Celsius, BlockFi, FTX... and each time, the people who had their keys were fine. The people who trusted someone else lost everything.

- This rule has fallen off a bit with the popularity of ETFs and financial products. But the core truth hasn't changed. Trusted third parties are security holes. Get your bitcoin off of exchanges.

- **[ONE-LINER DROP]:** If someone else holds the keys, it's their bitcoin. You just have a promise. And we've seen what promises are worth.

---

## RULE 2: Don't Trust, Verify (2-3 min)

**[TALKING POINTS]**

- This one goes beyond bitcoin. This is a life rule. But it starts here.

- You should not be taking things at face value. Do not trust what you read without rationalizing it for yourself. Always look for counter arguments. Read all the sources and then weigh each as unbiased as you can.

- Bitcoin is all about removing trust. It is a trustless monetary system and peer-to-peer value transfer invention. And as such, one of the core golden rules is to be just as vigilant when learning about Bitcoin and trusting anyone for that matter.

- It is best practice to assume that everyone is a scammer trying to take your money. You should do your own research and don't just blindly believe anything that you hear. That includes anything that I say. Because most people don't know anything.

- As a part of this rule, we bitcoiners stand by the core belief that trustless and trust-minimized protocols are superior to human social structures.

- By being skeptical, it means you are a rational thinker and don't just blindly accept things as they are. Which is an integral part of bitcoin, and a foundational value of all bitcoiners.

- **[ONE-LINER DROP]:** The whole point of Bitcoin is that you don't have to trust anyone. So why would you start now?

---

## RULE 3: Start Small (2 min)

**[TALKING POINTS]**

- We believe very strongly... we put our money on the line... that Bitcoin is the superior store of value. But it must be emphasized that without conviction, newcomers will likely sell at the wrong time or mess up their custody.

- Allocation to Bitcoin increases with knowledge. Learning about Bitcoin and being curious will naturally increase your holdings. But going all in at the very beginning is not aligned with the analytical Bitcoin ethos.

- Start with an amount you're comfortable with. Learn how wallets work. Make mistakes with small amounts. Send a test transaction. Get the feel for it.

- Conviction grows with understanding. The more you learn, the more you'll want to stack. That's how it works for every single bitcoiner I've ever met. Nobody starts at 100%. You earn your conviction.

- **[ONE-LINER DROP]:** Bitcoin isn't going anywhere. You have time. Learn first, stack later.

---

## RULE 4: Stay Humble (3-4 min)

**[TALKING POINTS -- this is the meatiest rule, take your time]**

- Matt Odell constantly reminds everyone to stay humble. And I think it's a value that all of us aspire to but struggle to maintain. Ironically, I find myself being the most humble after Bitcoin humbles me in a bear market. It's the bull markets where you really have to check yourself.

- So what does being humble actually mean?

- **First: never talk about your holdings.** There's a great saying... "Always talk about Bitcoin but never talk about your Bitcoins." Bitcoin the mission is incredibly important to talk about. But treating Bitcoin like a get-rich scheme undercuts the message and makes you a target.

- **Second: don't overextend.** Because of bitcoin's free market nature, being greedy can have serious negative consequences. There's no one to come and save you if you mess up. Dollar cost average. Lump sum on dips. Use limit orders instead of trying to trade around the volatility.

  - I coined the phrase "buy the dip, not the salsa." Meaning buy the price dips... not the spicy hot run-ups.

- **Third: no leverage.** It's highly advised not to leverage yourself dangerously. Bitcoin is a sound money system, not a credit system, for a reason. And bitcoiners are highly skeptical of credit in general.

- **Fourth: don't fall for shitcoins.** Being greedy has the connotation that you are trying to acquire more dollars rather than trying to acquire more Bitcoin. That's why it's so vital not to fall for the scams. They're all decentralized in name only and follow the "build fast and break things" philosophy instead of the slow and steady wins the race bitcoiner philosophy.

- **Fifth: control your emotions.** No fomoing into price movements. The price will always come back down. Don't try to time the market.

- **[ONE-LINER DROP]:** Stay humble or Bitcoin will humble you. And trust me... Bitcoin is a much better teacher than I am.

## RULE 5: Stack Sats and HODL (2-3 min)

**[TALKING POINTS]**

- While everyone has life events and emergencies that come up... I personally never plan on selling my bitcoin. Bitcoin is money. It should be used for payments, not sold to diversify, not sold to buy luxuries. Bitcoin is meant to be used, not treated as an investment to pay capital gains taxes on.

- There's a certain self-sacrificial nature of bitcoiners. We believe in the decentralized movement... to both support bitcoin's price for the good of the many, but to also think long term. Sacrifice today to be better off tomorrow.

- The movement isn't about buying dollars. It's about stacking sats. Preserving your wealth. And passing it down to future generations.

- This is the part that most people don't get. We're not trying to get rich. We're trying to opt out. We're trying to build something that outlasts us.

- **[ONE-LINER DROP]:** Everyone has a price they wished they had bought at. Don't be the person who has a price they wished they hadn't sold at.

## RULE 6: Laser Eyes Until Fiat Dies (2 min)

**[TALKING POINTS]**

- There was a meme that laser eyes were only until $100K... in which case everyone still better have them on. But I like the rhyme, and I think it's about more than a profile picture.

- It's about being laser focused on the vision. No distractions. Laser eyes are a superpower. It's symbolic of how having bitcoin is like having a superpower... having your wealth in a futuristic super technology.

- It's a reminder about what we're here to do. Of the centuries-long project in which we're building. And that the ultimate goal is to return the world to a sound money standard.

- This is the rule that ties all the others together. Self-custody. Skepticism. Patience. Humility. Long-term thinking. It all points in one direction. And we don't stop until we get there.

- **[ONE-LINER DROP]:** The mission doesn't end at a price target. It ends when money is honest again.
