## Part 2: Source Inventory

### The Three Bull Cases Framework

1. Bull Case 1 -- The Successor Thesis: Bitcoin replaces the dollar as the global settlement layer. Rules, not rulers. (Larry Lepard, Nik Bhatia, maximalists.) (Author)
2. Bull Case 2 -- The Debasement Thesis: Governments print, 21 million stays fixed, price goes up. Math on autopilot. (Lyn Alden, Luke Gromen, macro crowd.) (Author)
3. Bull Case 3 -- System-Agnostic Money: Bitcoin functions across blocs without replacing any system. Captures the interoperability premium. (Author -- original thesis)
4. Bull Case 3 is the most robust because it survives scenarios where the other two fail. (Author)

### Monetary Balkanization (The Setup)

5. The Eurodollar system ($13-80T in offshore dollar-denominated obligations) is too massive and self-reinforcing to be replaced by any alternative settlement system. (Snider framework)
6. The dollar wasn't chosen by policy -- it emerged because markets needed deep, liquid, flexible infrastructure. No competitor comes close. (Snider)
7. The yuan isn't freely convertible. China maintains capital controls. You can't be the world's reserve currency and maintain capital controls simultaneously. (Snider / Author)
8. The system is dying from internal contradictions -- dollar scarcity triggers defaults that destroy the credit that created dollar demand. (Snider -- "dollar shortage" thesis)
9. Luke Gromen's gold-for-oil-yuan pathway: sell dollars, buy gold, ship to China, sell for yuan, buy Iranian oil. "De facto the end of the dollar's post-71 status." (Gromen)
10. The stock-vs-flow problem: all oil trade is $2-3T/year vs $13-80T in outstanding Eurodollar obligations. Even if all oil switched to yuan, the stock dwarfs the flow. (Author synthesis)
11. Lyn Alden holds both positions simultaneously: long tail on dollar (Snider's point) AND terminal phase (Gromen's point). Most intellectually honest framing. (Alden)
12. Nobody in the nine Iran transcripts addresses the possibility that both are true: the dollar is dying AND nothing will replace it. (Author)

### The Interregnum (The Outcome)

13. The Interregnum: the period between monetary orders where no system has enough network effect to dominate. Not a transition TO something -- a prolonged state OF nothing. (Author -- named concept)
14. Historical precedent: 1914-1944 between classical gold standard and Bretton Woods. 30 years of competing blocs, capital controls, trade fragmentation, monetary chaos. Everyone got poorer. (Author)
15. Open question: does a new hegemon emerge to end the interregnum (China? digital SDR? US via AI productivity?) or does fragmentation persist? Duration uncertain; dynamics during it are not. (Author)
16. The scenario nobody in Bitcoin-adjacent macro wants to talk about because it doesn't have a clean narrative attached to it. (Author)

### System-Agnostic Money (The Bull Case)

17. If fragmentation is the endgame, the assets that win aren't the ones betting on a specific new system (yuan, gold-backed SDR, Eurodollar continuation). They're the ones that are system-agnostic. (Author)
18. Gold qualifies as system-agnostic money. But Bitcoin does it with telecommunications-speed settlement. (Author)
19. Lyn Alden's Broken Money thesis: Bitcoin closes the telecommunications-settlement mismatch that gave banks their monopoly since the 1860s. That mismatch gets WORSE in a fragmented system with competing blocs and capital controls. (Alden, Broken Money)
20. The interoperability premium: Bitcoin operates across all blocs without permission from any of them. (Author -- named concept)
21. "Bitcoin replaces the dollar" is a story. "Number go up" is a trade. "Bitcoin functions in the cracks between dying systems" is just... infrastructure. Not exciting. But most likely. (Author)

### Robustness Analysis

22. If the dollar reasserts (Snider) -- Bull Cases 1 and 2 weaken, but 3 holds as long as any fragmentation persists. (Author)
23. If Bitcoin never becomes the settlement layer (Simon Dixon: "Bitcoin doesn't fix it") -- Bull Case 1 fails, but 3 doesn't need systemic reform, only systemic dysfunction. (Author / Dixon)
24. If debasement is slower than expected (Lyn's decade timeline) -- Bull Case 2 is less urgent, but 3 operates on any timeline where blocs are competing. (Author / Alden)
25. Bull Case 3 only requires belief that the system fragments. Every thinker across the ideological spectrum provides evidence it is. The disagreement is speed and mechanism, not direction. (Author)

### Real-Time Evidence (Iran Crisis, March-April 2026)

26. Snider's April 9 "Asia Is Running Out of Dollars": Taiwan, Indonesia, India experienced dollar funding crunches during the oil shock. Crisis intensified dollar demand, not weakened it. (Snider, Eurodollar University)
27. TIPS-derived inflation expectations declined despite $100+ oil -- markets priced demand destruction, not runaway inflation. (Snider, "Oil-driven deflation" Mar 28)
28. Gold fell ~12% initially alongside equities -- monetary tightening signal, contradicting Luke's expectation of gold mooning via gold-for-oil-yuan. (Snider / market data)
29. Iran's crypto toll system (Bitcoin + USDT for Strait passage, ~$2M per VLCC, $600-800M/month) is a live example of system-agnostic money functioning at nation-state scale during exactly the kind of crisis this thesis describes. (Bloomberg, CoinDesk, Fortune)
30. The war proved both sides right simultaneously: dollar demand surged (Snider) AND alternative margins grew (Gromen). The system didn't transition. It fragmented. (Author synthesis)

### Scope Distinctions

31. 9.6 assumes Bitcoin BECOMES the foundation (new Bretton Woods). 9.10 assumes NO foundation emerges -- Bitcoin works BETWEEN systems. (Author -- scope note)
32. 9.8 is about inevitability via debasement math. 9.10 is about Bitcoin's unique positioning in a specific macro outcome (fragmentation) that the debasement thesis doesn't fully address. (Author -- scope note)
33. Note overlap with 3.1 Block 19 "The Eurodollar Trap" -- 3.1 covers the Eurodollar system as a fiat problem. 9.10 uses it as setup for the fragmentation/Bitcoin thesis. Different angle, same underlying mechanism. (Cross-ref)
