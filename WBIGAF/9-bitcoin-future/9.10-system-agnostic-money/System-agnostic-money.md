System-Agnostic Money -- The Third Bitcoin Bull Case

The two bull cases everyone knows:
1. The Successor Thesis -- Bitcoin replaces the dollar as the global settlement layer. Rules, not rulers. New monetary base layer. (Larry Lepard, Nik Bhatia, most maximalists)
2. The Debasement Thesis -- Governments print, 21 million stays fixed, number go up. The math is on autopilot. (Lyn Alden, Luke Gromen, macro-Bitcoin crowd)

The one nobody names:
3. System-Agnostic Money -- Bitcoin doesn't need to replace the dollar. Doesn't need to become the settlement layer. Just needs to function across blocs. The asset that works whether you're in the dollar bloc, the yuan bloc, or the fragmented middle.
This is the tick-tock, next block thesis, that honeybadger don't care, that all bitcoin has to do is outlast the system to win. 

---

The setup: Monetary Balkanization

- The Eurodollar system ($13-80T in offshore dollar-denominated obligations) is too massive and self-reinforcing to be replaced by gold-for-oil-yuan or any alternative settlement system
- Jeff Snider's argument: the dollar wasn't chosen by policy, it emerged because markets needed deep, liquid, flexible infrastructure. No competitor comes close. Yuan isn't even freely convertible.
- But the system is also dying from internal contradictions -- dollar scarcity triggers defaults that destroy the credit that created dollar demand in the first place
- Luke Gromen's gold-for-oil-yuan pathway is real but the stock of dollar claims dwarfs the flow -- all oil trade is $2-3T/year vs $13-80T in outstanding obligations
- Lyn Alden holds both positions simultaneously: long tail on the dollar AND terminal phase. Most honest framing.
- Nobody addresses the possibility that both are true: the dollar system is dying AND nothing will replace it

The outcome: The Interregnum

- Period between monetary orders where no system has enough network effect to dominate
- Not a transition TO something -- a prolonged state OF nothing
- Historical precedent: 1914-1944 between classical gold standard and Bretton Woods. 30 years of competing blocs, capital controls, trade fragmentation, monetary chaos. Everyone got poorer.
- No clean trade. No clean narrative. Just degraded infrastructure.

The bull case: Interoperability Premium

- If the system fragments rather than transitions, the assets that win aren't the ones betting on a specific new system
- They're the ones that are system-agnostic -- functioning across blocs without permission from any of them
- Gold qualifies. But Bitcoin does it with telecommunications-speed settlement.
- Lyn Alden's Broken Money thesis: Bitcoin closes the telecommunications-settlement mismatch that gave banks their monopoly since the 1860s. That mismatch gets WORSE in a fragmented system with competing blocs and capital controls.
- The bull case: "Bitcoin wins because nothing replaces the dollar, and in the resulting fragmentation, the asset that operates across all blocs captures the interoperability premium"

Why this is the most robust bull case:

- If the dollar reasserts (Snider's implicit view) -- Bull Cases 1 and 2 weaken, but 3 holds as long as ANY fragmentation persists
- If Bitcoin never becomes the settlement layer (Simon Dixon: "Bitcoin doesn't fix it") -- Bull Case 1 fails, but 3 doesn't need systemic reform, only systemic dysfunction
- If debasement is slower than expected (Lyn's decade timeline) -- Bull Case 2 is less urgent, but 3 operates on any timeline where blocs are competing
- It only requires you to believe the system fragments. Everyone across the ideological spectrum provides evidence it is.

Real-time evidence (Iran crisis, March-April 2026):

- Snider's April 9 "Asia Is Running Out of Dollars" -- Taiwan, Indonesia, India all experienced dollar funding crunches during the oil shock. Crisis INTENSIFIED dollar demand, not weakened it.
- TIPS-derived inflation expectations declined despite $100+ oil -- markets priced demand destruction
- Gold fell ~12% initially alongside equities -- monetary tightening signal, contradicting Luke's expectation
- Iran's crypto toll system (Bitcoin + USDT for Strait passage) is a live example of system-agnostic money functioning at nation-state scale during exactly the kind of crisis this thesis describes
- The war proved BOTH sides right simultaneously: dollar demand surged (Snider) AND alternative margins grew (Gromen). The system didn't transition. It fragmented.

The uncomfortable truth:

"Bitcoin replaces the dollar" is a story.
"Number go up" is a trade.
"Bitcoin functions in the cracks between dying systems" is just... infrastructure.
It's not exciting. But it might be the most likely.
