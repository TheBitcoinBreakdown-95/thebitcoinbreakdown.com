## Part 2: Source Inventory

### A. 2008 Crisis Context

1. In 2008, Satoshi Nakamoto invented Bitcoin on the principle of freeing from the regulation and control of banks and middlemen (Author)
2. Bitcoin created out of the 2008 financial crisis — Satoshi foresaw that moral hazard and incentives from central bank intervention would cause an alteration in the fabric of financial perception (Will Clemente)
3. In the 15 years since, federal debt as % of GDP rose from 60% to 120% (Will Clemente)
4. "epic, worldwide financial crisis threatens to destroy the entire global economy" — governments unable to prevent it, only postpone consequences and worsen them in the process (Strolight)
5. Emergency measures taken by world governments, "flooding the economy to the tune of hundreds of billions of dollars in bailouts" (Author)
6. "our political and economic leaders barely save us from catastrophe, as the entire world is thrust into recession" (Author)

### B. October 31st Release

7. October 31, 2008 — whitepaper "Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System" published to the cryptography mailing list (Satoshi)
8. "A breakthrough was made, and an unstoppable idea was let loose on the world" (Gigi)
9. "An idea so profound, so outrageous, that it was ignored by almost everyone for the first couple of years — and is still dismissed by most" (Gigi)
10. "As Victor Hugo reminds us: nothing is as powerful as an idea whose time has come" (Gigi)
11. "we went from a world where digital cash was just an idea to a world where Bitcoin exists" (Gigi)
12. "with every passing block, Bitcoin reminds us that this idea is now a reality" (Gigi)
13. "A purely peer-to-peer version of electronic cash would allow online payments to be sent directly from one party to another without going through a financial institution." — Satoshi, opening line of the whitepaper (Satoshi)

### C. Bitcoin as Monetary Manifesto

14. The system known as 'Bitcoin' is specific — not just generic digital cash: "a predefined monetary schedule, 21 million units, based on proof of work" (Nic Carter)
15. "It's not just a way to move value through a communications medium. It's an entire monetary manifesto. An affront to the fiat system." (Nic Carter)
16. "In a time where overleveraged financial institutions had risked the solvency of the global economy, the seeds of rebellion were planted. A revolutionary new economic system was born." (Author)

### D. Protestant Reformation Parallel

17. Protestant Reformation responsible for social and political liberation in Europe (Bitcoin Magazine)
18. Benefits: weakening centralized political power (the Church), increase of decentralized church organization, seeding separation of church and state, printing the Bible in common languages enabling intellectual freedom (Bitcoin Magazine)
19. Martin Luther's "95 Theses" posted on the church door in Wittenberg on October 31, 1517 (Bitcoin Magazine)
20. Both Satoshi and Luther provided a spark to latent energy in people tired of "wholesale theft that defined the systems of their respective times" (Bitcoin Magazine)
21. Both chose October 31st for its symbolism of death and renewal (blog.bitcoin.org.hk)
22. Luther took offense with plenary indulgences — sinners reducing purgatory time through payment; considered "treasury of the church" keeping the lavish Vatican lifestyle afloat with the "sins" of the believers (blog.bitcoin.org.hk)
23. Both suggested getting rid of the central authority to "forgive us of our debt" (blog.bitcoin.org.hk)
24. Luther's ideas spread through the newly invented printing press (blog.bitcoin.org.hk)
25. The Reformation was "brutally fought over between 1618 and 1648, a time that killed as many as 30% of Europe's inhabitants" (blog.bitcoin.org.hk)
26. "Hopefully Bitcoin can spread around the world in a more peaceful way" (blog.bitcoin.org.hk)
27. Satoshi comparison to Gutenberg often made, but "a more accurate comparison to Satoshi would actually be Martin Luther" (Bitcoin Magazine)
28. Both provided "a way to opt out of the oppression in authority" (Bitcoin Magazine)

32. "Banks must be trusted to hold our money and transfer it electronically, but they lend it out in waves of credit bubbles with barely a fraction in reserve" (Satoshi)
33. "We have to trust them with our privacy, trust them not to let identity thieves drain our accounts" (Satoshi)
34. The Bitcoin whitepaper: "probably one of the most semantically dense documents in history. Not a word is wasted." (Nic Carter)
