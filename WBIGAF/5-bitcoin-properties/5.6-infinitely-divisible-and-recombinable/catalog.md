## Part 2: Source Inventory

### Divisibility and Economic Expansion

1. Bitcoin doesn't have to be inflationary if it is a debit-based system -- by being infinitely divisible, it can expand with the economy. (Author)
2. Bitcoin can theoretically store all of the wealth in the solar system. (Author)
3. In a debit-based system, you don't need inflation to expand the money supply as the economy grows -- you need an infinitely divisible money to subdivide and absorb the growing economy. (Author)
4. Anyone on Earth can afford a little piece of bitcoin. (Author)
5. Bitcoin enables microtransactions. (Author)

### Recombinable

6. Bitcoin can be put back together into larger units (recombination). (Author)
7. Recombination of monetary units has historically been difficult to do, which is what made metals so good as money. (Author)

### Satoshi Unit (Gemini)

8. A bitcoin can be subdivided into fractional coins, so its usefulness for payments is not constrained by limited supply. (Gemini)
9. The smallest unit of a bitcoin is called a Satoshi, named after Satoshi Nakamoto. (Gemini)
10. A Satoshi is one one-hundred millionth of a bitcoin (0.00000001). (Gemini)
11. Participants in the system can own less than a single coin -- far less than a single coin. (Gemini)
12. Divisibility enables commerce and transfers based on smaller notional values. (Gemini)

### Reddit FAQ

13. Each bitcoin can be divided down to 8 decimal places, so you don't have to worry about buying a whole bitcoin. (Reddit r/Bitcoin FAQ)
