## Part 2: Source Inventory

### Core Property

1. No one can prevent you from interacting with the Bitcoin network. (Author)
2. No one can censor, alter, or block transactions they disagree with. (Author)
3. See Operation Chokepoint as a real-world example of financial censorship. (Author)

### Why Bitcoin Is Not Anonymous (and Why That Doesn't Matter)

4. One of the earliest sources of demand for bitcoin was use in the illicit drug trade, leading many to believe Bitcoin was primarily valuable for its anonymity. (Vijay Boyapati)
5. Bitcoin is far from an anonymous currency -- every transaction is permanently recorded on a public blockchain. (Vijay Boyapati)
6. The historical record of blockchain transactions allows for later forensic analysis to identify the source of funds. (Vijay Boyapati)
7. Forensic blockchain analysis led to the apprehension of the perpetrator of the MtGox heist. (Vijay Boyapati)
8. A sufficiently careful person can conceal their identity when using Bitcoin, but anonymity is not the key reason Bitcoin was popular for illicit trade. (Vijay Boyapati)

### Permissionless Network as the Key Attribute

9. The key attribute making Bitcoin valuable for proscribed activities is that it is "permissionless" at the network level -- there is no human intervention deciding whether a transaction should be allowed. (Vijay Boyapati)
10. As a distributed peer-to-peer network, Bitcoin is by its very nature designed to be censorship-resistant. (Vijay Boyapati)

### Contrast With Fiat and Gold

11. In the fiat banking system, states regulate banks and gatekeepers of money transmission to report and prevent outlawed uses. (Vijay Boyapati)
12. Capital controls are a classic example of regulated money transmission -- a millionaire may find it very hard to transfer their wealth when fleeing an oppressive regime. (Vijay Boyapati)
13. Gold's physical nature makes it difficult to transmit at distance, making it far more susceptible to state regulation than Bitcoin. (Vijay Boyapati)
14. India's Gold Control Act is an example of state regulation of gold. (Vijay Boyapati)
