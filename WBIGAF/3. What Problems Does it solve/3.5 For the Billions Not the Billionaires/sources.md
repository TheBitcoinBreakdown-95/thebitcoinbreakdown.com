**Skeptical Perspectives:**

**Mohamad El Chamaa**, a journalist, expressed reservations, noting bitcoin's 70% decline over the past year and questioning whether crypto offers true stability compared to traditional assets.

**Ray Hindi**, a digital asset management executive, warned that many Lebanese citizens maintain false hope that the existing banking system will recover, continuing to hold worthless deposits.

**Broader Implications:**

The World Bank characterizes Lebanon's crisis as among the worst economic catastrophes globally since the 1850s. The International Monetary Fund is negotiating a $3 billion bailout, though political paralysis hampers governance reform efforts.

For desperate citizens, cryptocurrency represents neither investment opportunity nor ideological commitment — it's a survival mechanism when traditional financial systems fail entirely.

**Bitcoin ATMs:** Lebanon has six bitcoin ATMs — one in Aamchit and five in Beirut. Those who spoke with CNBC say that the optimal on-ramps to accessing bitcoin are either earning it (through mining or paid work), or buying it with tether.

## Link #5: New Documentary Shows How Bitcoin Helped Verify Guatemala's Presidential Elections (Status: DONE)

**Source:** Bitcoin Magazine

**URL:** https://bitcoinmagazine.com/culture/documentary-bitcoin-guatemalas-presidential-elections

**Note:** Content assembled from Bitcoin Magazine partial extraction + WebSearch + Open Dialogue Foundation + Coinotag mirrors.

### Content

**Documentary Title:** "Immutable Democracy" (available at film.simpleproof.com)

A documentary profiles how Bitcoin technology was utilized to bring integrity and transparency to Guatemala's 2023 presidential elections. The film demonstrates an innovative application of blockchain technology in electoral verification processes.

**Background & Context:**

Guatemala's Supreme Elections Tribunal (TSE) faced critical challenges in 2019 when its IT system crashed on election night, causing widespread confusion. The 2023 elections required a more robust solution to maintain democratic integrity amid political uncertainty and potential interference.

**The Electoral System:**

Guatemala's decentralized model relies on approximately 25,000 Vote Reception Boards (JRVs), staffed by roughly 100,000 citizen volunteers across voting centers. These boards manually count ballots under observation from political party representatives and international election observers, generating around 150,000 tally sheet images.

**Technical Implementation — OpenTimestamps:**

The TSE implemented Simple Proof, a Guatemalan tech startup, utilizing the OpenTimestamps protocol built on Bitcoin's blockchain. OpenTimestamps was created by Bitcoin Core developer Peter Todd.

The system works by "digitizing around 150,000 vote tally sheet images and saving them on a decentralized ledger," securing documents against alteration while remaining "open to anyone" for verification. By linking information to specific blockchain blocks, the system allows independent verification across thousands of network nodes.

**Rafael Cordon Quote (Simple Proof co-founder):** OpenTimestamps records evidence of documents on the Bitcoin blockchain in an immutable manner, protecting official election materials while providing defense against artificial intelligence-generated disinformation.

**Citizen Verification Portal:**

Citizens can access a dedicated web portal enabling them to verify timestamp evidence for every tally sheet from each election. While this doesn't guarantee individual tally sheet validity, it provides transparency by allowing residents to independently confirm when documents received their timestamps. The system helps identify potential irregularities — such as tally sheets timestamped significantly later than expected, which could signal problems.

Citizens independently downloaded and validated tally sheets, publishing findings on social media — crucial amid allegations of fraud and political meddling by the Attorney General's office.

**Impact After Arevalo's Victory:**

Following the unexpected victory of President-elect Bernardo Arevalo, allegations of fraud faced intense scrutiny. The blockchain verification system played a crucial role in debunking these claims through transparent, verifiable documentation.

**Documentary Quote:** "Why Bitcoin proves useful for elections... because all these communities share a common objective: establishing truth."

**Global Significance:**

Guatemala's model provides "a valuable blueprint for other nations seeking to enhance electoral transparency" amid growing AI and disinformation concerns. The principles of "decentralization, transparency, and community empowerment remain universally applicable."

The Open Dialogue Foundation nominated Bitcoin Simple Proof Solution for the Copenhagen Democracy Tech Award.

**The Cash Crisis (December 2022 — February 2023):**

The governor of the Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN), Godwin Emefiele, announced that by the end of January 2023 (later extended to February 10), Nigeria would fully transition from physical cash (naira) to eNaira. This included:
- Issuing new banknote designs
- Invalidating old notes
- Imposing severe ATM and bank withdrawal limits

**Catastrophic Consequences:**
- Half the nation was left with old, worthless banknotes
- Commuters were left without cash to pay for return transportation
- Many small businesses closed because customers had no money to pay
- The entire Nigerian financial system came to a standstill

Even after various government promotions, Nigerians were wary of the eNaira and refused to use it. Widespread confusion and a general mistrust of the ruling elite hampered adoption among the 218 million populace. Instead, citizens turned to peer-to-peer Bitcoin trading, driving a ~40% premium on Bitcoin prices in Nigeria.
