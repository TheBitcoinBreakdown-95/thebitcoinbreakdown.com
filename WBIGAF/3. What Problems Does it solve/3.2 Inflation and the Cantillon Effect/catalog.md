### Block 2: Meet Richard Cantillon

**THESIS:** Three hundred years ago, an economist figured out exactly how new money transfers wealth from the last receivers to the first — and he profited from his own insight while his debtors were destroyed.

**ARGUMENT TYPE:** Narrative/Anecdotal
**APPEAL:** Ethos

**ARGUMENT BODY:**
1. Richard Cantillon (c.1680s–1734): Irish-French economist, author of *Essai sur la Nature du Commerce en Général* — "the cradle of political economy"
2. Cantillon effects: the real changes in resource allocation between the time of new money creation and full price adjustment
3. First receivers spend at pre-inflation prices; last receivers face already-inflated prices
4. Cantillon personally profited by understanding John Law's Mississippi Company inflationary scheme — made a fortune while his debtors were ruined
5. He was pursued by lawsuits, criminal charges, and murder plots until his death
6. The effect is 300 years old — named by a man who lived and died by it. Nothing has changed.

The Cantillon Effect is not a theory discovered in a library — it was lived, exploited, and named by a man who profited from it three centuries ago. The mechanism he identified is exactly how the modern Federal Reserve operates, which is what the next block breaks down step by step.

### Block 8: The Rigged Ruler — How They Hide the Theft

**THESIS:** The government didn't reduce inflation — it changed how inflation is measured, systematically understating the real rate to reduce its own obligations and hide the true cost of money printing.

**ARGUMENT TYPE:** Logical/Deductive
**APPEAL:** Logos

**ARGUMENT BODY:**
1. CPI is a government basket — "extremely misleading, but isn't technically wrong"
2. Boskin Commission (1996): concluded CPI overstated inflation by 1.1 points/year
3. Three changes: substitution bias (steak→hamburger), hedonic quality adjustments, geometric weighting
4. Substitution: when steak gets expensive, BLS assumes you buy hamburger — redefines inflation to measure survival, not standard of living
5. Hedonic: same price but "better" computer = recorded price decrease — always pulls CPI downward
6. Under pre-1980 methodology (ShadowStats): inflation during 2020-2022 was ~15%, not the reported 7-9%
7. Price inflation is actually 5.3% from a baseline of -2.2% (technology deflation) — real rate is 7.5% when you account for the deflation that should be happening
8. Every percentage point of "reduced bias" saves the government billions in Social Security obligations
9. The ruler changed, not the reality

**CONCLUSION & TRANSITION:**
