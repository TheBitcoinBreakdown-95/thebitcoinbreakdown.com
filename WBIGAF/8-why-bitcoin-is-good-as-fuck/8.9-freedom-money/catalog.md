### Ludwig von Mises on Sound Money and Liberty

14. Ludwig von Mises argues that sound money is an instrument for the protection of civil liberties and a means of limiting government power (1912, The Theory of Money and Credit). (Mises)
15. "It is impossible to grasp the meaning of the idea of sound money if one does not realize that it was devised as an instrument for the protection of civil liberties against despotic inroads on the part of governments. Ideologically it belongs in the same class with political constitutions and bills of rights." (Mises)
16. "The excellence of the gold standard is to be seen in the fact that it renders the determination of the monetary unit's purchasing power independent of the policies of governments and political parties." (Mises)
17. "Parliamentary control of finances works only if the government is not in a position to provide for unauthorized expenditures by increasing the circulating amount of fiat money. Viewed in this light, the gold standard appears as an indispensable implement of the body of constitutional guarantees that make the system of representative government function." (Mises)
