## Part 2: Source Inventory

### Greg Foss: Bitcoin as CDS and Insurance (YouTube)

1. "There's no counterparty risk" -- Bitcoin is not an insurance company; when you hold it in self-custody, you don't need to worry about the insurance company failing. (Greg Foss)
2. When trading credit default swaps, Foss had exposure to Bear Stearns -- when Bear Stearns looked like it was going to fail, he had to run out and buy insurance on his insurance company. Bitcoin eliminates this circular problem. (Greg Foss)
3. "Bitcoin is so beautiful because there's no counterparty. So there's a way to value it and there's the way to appreciate that it has no counterparty risk." (Greg Foss)
4. Unlike gold, Bitcoin has a fixed supply, is transferable and portable, and is auditable via the blockchain -- "Bitcoin is so superior, as not only a technology, but also as an auditable asset." (Greg Foss)
5. From a credit trader's perspective, Bitcoin is "the most beautiful insurance policy of the failure of fiats globally." (Greg Foss)

### Foss's $700,000 Valuation Math

6. Foss views Bitcoin as insurance against sovereign debt default -- currently undervalued. (Foss / OpenAI summary)
7. Foss likens Bitcoin to credit default swaps (CDS) -- stating it could be worth 1/7 of its value for the USA alone. (Foss / OpenAI summary)
8. Bitcoin's unmanipulated market and various valuation perspectives contribute to its attractiveness as an investment opportunity. (Foss / OpenAI summary)
9. U.S. debt (funded and unfunded liabilities) is approximately $200 trillion -- Foss uses this as the insurance base. (Foss / OpenAI summary)
10. The 5-year CDS spread for the US is around 40 basis points (0.4%) -- for every $10 million of US debt, it costs $40,000 per year to insure against default. (Foss / OpenAI summary)
11. Extrapolating this CDS calculation across different time periods (like 20 years) and extending to other G7/G20 nations produces a total CDS value divided by the total number of Bitcoins in existence. (Foss / OpenAI summary)
12. By using this approach, Foss suggests Bitcoin could be worth over $700,000 per unit -- if it is considered as insurance against global debt. (Foss / OpenAI summary)
