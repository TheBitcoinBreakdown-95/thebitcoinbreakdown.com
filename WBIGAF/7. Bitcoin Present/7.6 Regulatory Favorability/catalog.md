### SAB 121 Repeal

14. SAB 121, issued by the SEC in 2022, forced banks to record Bitcoin held for customers as liabilities on their balance sheets. (Author unknown)
15. SAB 121 made Bitcoin custody extremely unattractive for regulated institutions because they had to hold capital reserves against these liabilities -- something not required for other assets. (Author unknown)
16. With SAB 121 repealed, banks are free to custody Bitcoin without punitive accounting treatment. (Author unknown)
17. BNY Mellon was identified as the first bank to receive an SEC exemption from SAB 121. (Unchained Crypto)
18. Traditional finance firms including JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, and Fidelity can now securely hold Bitcoin for institutions and individuals. (Author unknown)
19. SAB 121 repeal paves the way for Bitcoin as institutional collateral -- regulated institutions were previously discouraged from using Bitcoin as collateral due to onerous balance sheet requirements. (Author unknown)
20. Bitcoin-backed lending markets will expand, enabling institutions to borrow against BTC instead of selling it. (Author unknown)
21. SAB 121 repeal positions Bitcoin closer to replacing US Treasuries as a reserve asset. (Author unknown)
22. SAB 121 repeal enables Bitcoin to become an integral part of financial markets -- derivatives, repo markets, and institutional lending. (Author unknown)
