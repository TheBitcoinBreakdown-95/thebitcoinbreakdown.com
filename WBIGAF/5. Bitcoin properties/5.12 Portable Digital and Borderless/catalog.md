### Vijay Boyapati -- Portability Compared to Fiat and Gold

13. Bitcoins are the most portable store of value ever used by man -- private keys representing hundreds of millions of dollars can be stored on a tiny USB drive. (Vijay Boyapati)
14. Equally valuable sums can be transmitted between people on opposite ends of the Earth near-instantly. (Vijay Boyapati)
15. Fiat currencies are fundamentally digital and highly portable, but government regulations and capital controls mean large transfers usually take days or may not be possible at all. (Vijay Boyapati)
16. Cash can avoid capital controls, but the risk of storage and cost of transportation become significant for large amounts. (Vijay Boyapati)
17. Gold, being physical in form and incredibly dense, is by far the least portable -- it is no wonder the majority of bullion is never transported. (Vijay Boyapati)
18. When bullion is transferred between buyer and seller, it is typically only the title to gold that is transferred, not the physical bullion itself. (Vijay Boyapati)
19. Transmitting physical gold across large distances is costly, risky, and time-consuming. (Vijay Boyapati)
