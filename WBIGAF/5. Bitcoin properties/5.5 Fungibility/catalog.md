## Part 2: Source Inventory

### Gold as the Fungibility Standard

1. Gold provides the standard for fungibility -- when melted down, an ounce of gold is essentially indistinguishable from any other ounce, and gold has always traded this way on the market. (Vijay Boyapati)

### Fiat Fungibility Limitations

2. Fiat currencies are only as fungible as the issuing institutions allow them to be. (Vijay Boyapati)
3. A fiat banknote is usually treated like any other by merchants, but there are instances where large-denomination notes have been treated differently from small ones. (Vijay Boyapati)
4. India's government demonetized its 500 and 1000 rupee banknotes to stamp out the untaxed gray market, causing those notes to trade at a discount and making them no longer truly fungible with lower denomination notes. (Vijay Boyapati)

### Bitcoin Fungibility -- Network Level vs. Practical Level

5. Bitcoins are fungible at the network level -- every bitcoin, when transmitted, is treated the same on the Bitcoin network. (Vijay Boyapati)
6. Because bitcoins are traceable on the blockchain, a particular bitcoin may become tainted by its use in illicit trade. (Vijay Boyapati)
7. Merchants or exchanges may be compelled not to accept tainted bitcoins due to their transaction history. (Vijay Boyapati)
8. Without improvements to the privacy and anonymity of Bitcoin's network protocol, bitcoins cannot be considered as fungible as gold. (Vijay Boyapati)
