### Forward Compatibility (nakamoto.com)

38. Bitcoin supports signing transactions without broadcasting them -- the principle that any currently possible signed-but-not-broadcast transaction should remain valid and broadcastable. (nakamoto.com)
39. Transactions with nLocktime that are not valid for confirmation until a specified time could be used for inheritance or other time-delayed purposes. (nakamoto.com)
40. Bitcoin's commitment to forward compatibility gives everyone confidence in the protocol -- anyone can secure their funds by whatever scheme they dream up without needing permission. (nakamoto.com)

### Pierre Rochard -- Monetary Uncertainty

41. "The utility of a monetary system is an inverse function of the uncertainty experienced by its users. Bitcoin is the least uncertain monetary system in human history as a result of its radical decentralization." (Pierre Rochard)
42. People hold money to hedge against future uncertainty -- this is why it is so crucial for a monetary system to optimize for minimizing its own uncertainty. (Pierre Rochard)
43. Bitcoin's architecture is ruthlessly optimized for reliability under adversarial conditions: it assumes no trust, uses physical energy as a Sybil-resistant signal, requires no leader, no synchronized clocks, and no identity system. (Pierre Rochard)
44. Bitcoin converges on consensus through probabilistic finality rooted in objective cost. (Pierre Rochard)
