## Part 2: Source Inventory

### Bitcoin vs. Crypto Market Manipulation

1. At first it seems ironic to call bitcoin an intervention-free market -- crypto is the wild west of finance, rife with wash trading, price manipulation, exit liquidity scams, and rug pulls. (Author)
2. When it comes to bitcoin specifically, whales can buy and sell to move the price up and down, but that is basically all they can do. (Author)

### Structural Protections

3. Bitcoin is a debit-based system -- all credit comes from additional layers and won't fundamentally affect the main asset. (Author)
4. The supply is perfectly inelastic, which means no one can make more of it even when the price goes up. (Author)
5. No one can acquire bitcoin unless they do the work mining it or earn it from someone else. (Author)
6. Bitcoin is the hardest asset to confiscate, so the only real way to acquire it is by providing a service. (Author)
7. There are no banks to bail out, no lender of last resort, and no moral hazard in bitcoin. (Author)

### Price Volatility as a Feature

8. The price crashes right away when bitcoin is over-leveraged, and it has gotten less and less volatile over time as weak hands sell to strong hands. (Author)
9. Because the price crashes right away, there is no way for excess leverage to build up in the system -- it gets flushed out regularly, avoiding systematic risk. (Author)
10. It is extremely hard to suppress bitcoin's price the way gold has been suppressed, especially because of its digital nature and transparent monetary policy. (Author)
11. The supply of gold is unknown and difficult to verify or take custody of -- bitcoin's supply is fully auditable. (Author)
12. Price volatility builds character -- frequent clearing of leverage keeps the system from exploding and builds market participants who can withstand volatility. (Author)

### Global Peg / Measuring Stick

13. Bitcoin's case for why it is a 10x value to society is that it puts a peg to a global economy that has no peg anywhere in sight for any country. (Author, citing YouTube source)
14. Bitcoin is an incorruptible technology that can be used as a measuring stick -- a global money that is completely neutral, hard-capped, and transparent. (Author)

### Bitcoin Magazine -- Limits on Power

15. Billionaires and dictators can buy a lot of bitcoin, but they cannot control the system as they can with fiat money. (Bitcoin Magazine)
16. Unlike with the dollar, euro, or yuan model, they cannot tweak issuance, censor transactions, make special rules or bailouts for the aristocracy, or perform mass remote confiscation or debasement. (Bitcoin Magazine)

### Volatility as Growing Pains

17. Being a young currency and technology means that bitcoin's value fluctuates as it continues to find market-fit. (Netcoins)
18. Many people see bitcoin's volatility as the growing pains of a new and better financial system being put in place. (Netcoins)
19. Eventually, when the dust settles, people believe bitcoin's value will stabilize. (Netcoins)
