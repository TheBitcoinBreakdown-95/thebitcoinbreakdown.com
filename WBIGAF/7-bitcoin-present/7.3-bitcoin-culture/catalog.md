### Convicted Holders ("Psychopaths Who Will Never Sell")

3. Bitcoin holders exhibit the Endowment Effect -- they overvalue what they own. (Author unknown)
4. "We just spent a brutal bear market bleeding BTC into the hands of religious zealots who plan never to sell." (BitPaine)
5. The conviction of holders is stronger than ever and only growing. (Bitcoin Magazine)
6. Bitcoin keeps getting accumulated by strong hands who are convinced of the long-term value. (Bitcoin Magazine)
7. Even in periods of severe downturn, holders continue to stay resilient. (Bitcoin Magazine)
8. Long-term holders, even during downturns when they are profitable, have not been selling. (Bitcoin Magazine)

### The Annoying Minority (Minority Rule)

9. Chapter 2 of Nassim Taleb's "Skin in the Game" develops the Minority Rule -- a phenomenon that enables a stubborn minority to assert its preference on the broader population. (Nassim Taleb via Swan Bitcoin)
10. The Minority Rule provides a framework for understanding how Bitcoin Maximalists are influencing our financial lives. (Swan Bitcoin)
11. Ten million Bitcoiners are acting as an intransigent minority. (Swan Bitcoin)

### Bitcoin as a Movement

12. Bitcoin is one of the only assets with a revolution behind it. (Author unknown)
13. Bitcoin has its own "fed put" -- the mass movement that will buy the dip. (Author unknown)
14. ETH has no convicted natural buyers of last resort as Bitcoin does; it's held by traders and high-time-preference speculators. (Author unknown, via X)
