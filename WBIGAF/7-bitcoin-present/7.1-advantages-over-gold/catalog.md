## Part 2: Source Inventory

### Gold's Historical Failure

1. Gold was captured by governments and objectively failed as a sound monetary system due to its shortcomings. (Author unknown)
2. Claims on gold allowed banks to devalue their currency and depeg the gold standard. (Author unknown)
3. Gold failed because it is difficult to call bluff on the paper gold market. (Author unknown)
4. It is difficult, expensive, and cumbersome to self-custody gold. (Author unknown)
5. Gold is difficult and dangerous to sell in large quantities. (Author unknown)
6. Gold is defended with human life and has a history of bloodshed. (Author unknown)
7. Gold mining is far worse for the environment and can be sourced unethically. (Author unknown)

### Verifiability and Custody

8. "How is $16T of gold verifiable?" (Author unknown)
9. Imagine paying someone in gold -- they would question if it's real and how much it's worth, whereas Bitcoin has mobile wallets that verify transactions and easily transfer value. (Author unknown)
10. Bitcoin is succeeding because it is easy to call bluff on the paper BTC market. (Author unknown)
11. Gold gets progressively harder to custody; moving $100B of gold out of the market to verify is almost impossible. With Bitcoin, custody is as easy to move out $1,000 as $1T. (Author unknown)
12. Bitcoin can be cryptographically proven; smart contracts can lock up Bitcoin and you can keep track of it easily. (Author unknown)

### Scarcity: Bitcoin vs. Gold vs. Fiat

13. "The attribute that most clearly distinguishes Bitcoin from fiat currencies and gold is its predetermined scarcity. By design, at most 21 million bitcoins can ever be created." (Vijay Boyapati)
14. An owner of 10 bitcoins would know that at most 2.1 million people on earth (less than 0.03% of world population) could ever have as many bitcoins as they have. (Vijay Boyapati)
15. Gold, while scarce, is not immune to supply increases -- sea-floor or asteroid mining could cause dramatic supply rises. (Vijay Boyapati)
16. Fiat currencies have proven prone to constant increases in supply; nation-states persistently inflate money supply to solve short-term political problems. (Vijay Boyapati)

### Bitcoin's Design Improvements over Gold

17. Bitcoin was designed to specifically fix the shortcomings of gold: portability, divisibility, and verifiability. (Author unknown)
18. The goal is to transfer value across space, time, and scales. Gold is great at transporting across time but bad at space and scales. (Author unknown)
19. As a thought experiment: imagine a base metal as scarce as gold but boring, not useful for any practical or ornamental purpose, and with one magical property -- it can be transported over a communications channel. (Author unknown)
20. Bitcoin is a fully private monetary network -- for the first time in history, the true separation of money and state. (Author unknown)
21. Unlike gold, which fits inside a debt-based system, Bitcoin is a network in and of itself; its layers provide unlimited velocity and instant transfer. (Author unknown)
22. Being digital makes Bitcoin extremely portable, frictionless, and easily integrated into our digital world. (TBB blog)
23. Bitcoin is programmable and can be designed around without permission -- smart contracts, multi-party custody, payment channels, atomic swaps, time locks, vaults, micropayments, money streaming, insurance contracts, and more. (TBB blog)
24. With Bitcoin, AI, robots, self-driving cars, drones, and smart devices can hold and use Bitcoin in a robot economy independent of ours. (TBB blog)
25. This is not possible in the current credit-based system, which requires personal information and creditworthiness; Bitcoin can be settled instantly with cash finality. (TBB blog)

### Paper Bitcoin vs. Paper Gold

26. "The price suppression is a serious concern... The approval of futures ETFs is proof that Wall Street felt a need to go to their old standby tool." (CryptoMoses)
27. "With BTC, being a digital asset, if the market gets too skewed by futures manipulation, settlement is practical and essentially lets the 'bluff to be called', which I think will keep the market more honest." (CryptoMoses)

### Small Savers: Bitcoin vs. Gold

28. When a normal wage earner tries to save in gold, they pay a 68.6% premium buying 1-gram bars vs. the spot price ($99.34 vs. $58.92 on Apmex). (BritishHodl)
29. The small gold buyer must wait for gold to go up 67% before breaking even, while holding an asset where the industry is incentivized to keep price low enough for retail. (BritishHodl)
30. There is no finality of scarcity on gold -- as price goes up, more miners enter and the price gets driven down. (BritishHodl)

### Scalability

31. The blockchain scaling critique is nullified because different layers could settle back to the main chain; theoretically, trillions could settle in a single block. The network scales with value in a way gold cannot. (Author unknown)
32. Satoshi designed Bitcoin as a new gold. (Author unknown)

### Bitcoin and Gold as Complements

33. "In the end, Bitcoin and gold will co-exist. Similar to silver and gold, a bi-sound money standard consisting of gold and Bitcoin reserves will be common throughout the 21st century." (Author unknown)
34. Gold's tradeoff is that it's physical, limiting transfer by weight and energy. Bitcoin's tradeoff is block size, limiting number of transfers but not volume. (Author unknown)
35. If sending 1,000 BTC equals $1T, a small 40vb transaction can accommodate an enormous amount of value. (Author unknown)
36. "There's only one physical gold and only one digital gold. They aren't enemies -- they just have different tradeoffs. Combined, they're better than the sum of their parts." (Author unknown)
