Full **inspiration-first lesson** using all the principles from Phase 3. This won’t just _convey content_ — it will spark **curiosity**, foster **agency**, and plant seeds for **deep thinking**.
 
**🧭** **LESSON FRAMEWORK:** **Inspiration-First Design**  
Let’s assume the subject is **Economics**, for high school or early college students — but this can be adapted to **any subject**. The topic will be:  
**“What is money — and what happens if people stop trusting it?”**
 
**🎯** **Lesson Title:** **The Collapse of Trust: What is Money, Really?**
 
**🧩** **PART 1: IGNITE — The Emotional + Intellectual Hook**  
**🎥** **Open with:**  
A short video montage of hyperinflation in Zimbabwe, breadlines in Venezuela, and scenes from the Great Depression — no narration, just images, haunting music, and flashes of old and new currency.  
**Prompt after video**:  
“All of this happened — and is still happening. But why? What broke? What actually _is_ money — and what happens when people stop believing in it?”
 
**🔎** **PART 2: EXPLORE — The Burning Question**  
**Central Inquiry Question**:  
“If everyone woke up tomorrow and agreed that paper dollars had no value, what would happen? What _is_ money without trust?”
 
**🧪** **Quick “Think–Pair–Share”:**

- 1 minute silent reflection.
- 3-minute partner discussion.
- 2 volunteers share wild theories with class.

🧠 **Teacher Note**: The goal is not to correct — it’s to surface _curiosity_ and _misconceptions_. Let the fog hang.
 
**🧰** **PART 3: BUILD CONTEXT — But Only What They Need**  
**Short Interactive Lecture (10–12 minutes):**

|   |   |
|---|---|
|**Concept**|**Teaching Method**|
|Barter → Money Evolution|Use physical props or images to show trade items|
|Trust → Fiat Money|Play a brief clip from _The Big Short_ or Ray Dalio|
|Inflation vs Hyperinflation|Draw a simple graph, show grocery store receipts over time|
|Bitcoin & Decentralized Systems|Optional teaser — no answers, just hints|
 
⚠️ Keep it short, visual, and with pauses. Ask _“Does this surprise you?”_ or _“Why do you think this happened?”_
 
**🧗** **PART 4: CHALLENGE — A Real-World Roleplay Scenario**  
**Scenario:**  
“It’s 2030. The dollar has collapsed. Trust is gone. People are panicking. You are a _Monetary Task Force_, assigned to rebuild trust in a new system.”  
Break students into groups and assign roles:

- Economists
- Central Bankers
- Tech Startups
- Local Business Owners
- Journalists

**Task**:

Design a new currency system. Choose:

- What backs your currency?
- How do you build trust in it?
- Who controls it — the people? The government? An algorithm?
- How do you prevent collapse again?
 
**🧮** **Tools & Time:**

- 20–25 minutes team work
- Access to whiteboards or collaborative slides
- Optional: introduce constraints (e.g., limited tech, public distrust, time pressure)
 
**🎤** **PART 5: REFLECT AND PRESENT**  
Each team shares their plan (3–5 minutes each).  
As they present:

- Have audience members ask **Socratic questions** (e.g., “What happens if the internet goes down?” “Who audits the algorithm?”)
 
**🧠** **PART 6: DEBRIEF — Personal Connection & Wonder**  
**Exit Journal Prompt**:

- “What surprised you today?”
- “What do you now believe about money that you didn’t before?”
- “What are you still wondering about?”

Bonus prompt: _“Can money exist without people?”_
 
**🧱** **BONUS (For Homework or Next Class)**  
**Optional follow-up**:  
Watch a short YouTube explainer on Bitcoin or monetary policy.  
Students write their own “Explain Like I’m 5” summary of what money is — using only metaphors or stories.
 
**✅** **Recap: Why This Lesson Inspires**

|   |   |
|---|---|
|**Inspiration Principle**|**How It’s Used**|
|Burning Question|“What happens when money dies?”|
|Emotional Hook|Real-life collapse footage|
|Identity Connection|Students _create_ a system based on values|
|Autonomy|Choose solutions, debate trade-offs|
|Inquiry|No initial answers — only questions|
|Mission|Fix the monetary system|
|Surprise|Money isn’t backed by gold! Fiat explained|
|Peer Voice|Presentations, questioning, synthesis|
|Emotion|Tension, stakes, moral dimension|
