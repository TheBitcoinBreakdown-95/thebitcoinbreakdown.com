I could technically start this story at 2008 with the financial crisis and me getting into politics
 
Back in probably 2012 I was in high school at the time, and I had a friend over my house who was showing off how he could access the dark web and all of the shit you could do. He told me about all the .onion sites he knew about, all of the forums and honestly lists of things that should never be repeated. He told me about how he got his mushrooms off of some site called the silk road, and how there was a whole bitcoin economy, this internet money, of which you had to participate in if you were going to buy stuff. At the time I thought it was cool, but wasn't really impressed at all with this new money, I didn't really consider how hard it was to create a new money, just that it existed and yeah I guess there had to be a money that they used that wasn't trackable like a credit card. But beyond that I didn't think much of it.  
Fast forward to 2015 when  
I went to Cato University
   

Tell the story about this kid at a backyard house party, I bought CGC at $40 about a thousand bucks, and now it's at like $8, I felt horrible and cheated, and I honestly stopped investing my money for a long time, it turned me off to it, I was always so conservative with my money, with bitcoin it took me years of knowing about it to finally have the confidence in it
 
Don't forget about your teenage conservative years where you were super into politics, and were terrified about governments breaking the lower zero bound of interest rates by outlawing cash, and forcing you to keep your money in the bank. It was just about the start of bitcoin and I had no idea it was a thing yet, but I already knew what was happening, I already knew the risks
   

And probably, most importantly  
Bitcoin came at just the right time for me  
It was deep in quarantine where I was working from home, I was tired of all of my friends and family and I was severely depressed. I was completely lost, I hated my job and I didn’t know where my career was going. I only started advertising because I had nothing else I could do or liked doing. I was working endless hours only to eat and fall asleep and do it again. I was incapable of making myself happy, all of the girls I met with from apps were garbage and I was still hopelessly in love with someone I’ll never see again who was terrible for me. I had nothing except weed and death metal. Thank god for btbam holy shit. But nonetheless, bitcoin came at a very special time in my life, where I had a new interest in something political which wasn’t ruined by idiots or Trump. I had almost lost hope in humanity and the future, but when bitcoin pumped in 21 I actually took the time to learn what dad had bought, what I actually had in my name. And what I was investing in. I remember telling myself, I don't really know what I'm putting my money into, so I decided to do a little research. And it was all downhill from there. I have been endlessly obsessed ever since, bitcoin gave me a reason to be excited for the future, it gave me a purpose for life that I had been searching for.
    
Also, someone defined what Bitcoin class you're in by the date you bought and HODL'd so for me that would be the class of 17 technically, even though I didn't know what I was doing back then. 2012 is when I heard about it, and 2015 is when I first learned about the mining process, but assumed it was just a fad so I didn't invest anything. But I could have been a class of 15 wow.  
2020 dad got me back interested, and 2021 is when I really figured out what was going on.
 
I lack confidence in my beliefs and constantly question everything I know, I'm usually afraid to share my opinions because I realize that everything is nuanced. Bitcoin is the first time I've ever been sure about something, and it took me hundreds of hours of research to get there.
    
I buy bitcoin because I believe in its fundamentals, I believe in its ability to subvert the state and its pure form of property ownership. I love bitcoin because it was the thing that started it all, and it's ingenious design and architecture. I believe in bitcoin because it does one thing and does it really well. I trust bitcoin because it isn't going away any time soon, and if it for some reason had no further development on it whatsoever, it would still be one of the top digital assets ever made.
 
I think my prior knowledge of bitcoin helped me accurately depict the tipping point.  
The tipping point was probably a lot earlier, historians might say it was inevitable from the start,  
literally inevitable by design.  
But in my own risk vs reward way, I think I noticed the asymmetric opportunity. That's all this is, an asymmetric understanding of the market. It's very normal and it happens all the time in the world. People get convinced about something, they act on it, when they're right they get rewarded. I don't know why I think deep down that I'm always wrong about everything. I don't know if it's an internal socrates or some deep rooted self-esteem issues,
      

Why I love bitcoin  
My whole life up until this point, all of the politics I've studied, all of the economics I've learned, philosophy, technology, all of my viewpoints and most passionate values all connect to this one beautiful invention. It’s a really good feeling to know that what you end up doing with your life stems from all of your younger passions. It's almost like destiny, how all of my stars aligned. I kind of feel compelled to help it along, like I'm in the first generation.
 
When you participate, and especially when you are passionate, you are actively working to change the world, whereas a lot of other things political tend to be complete bullshit. If I was going to spend my precious hours on this earth dedicated towards a project I most believe in, it would be for bitcoin. I honestly don't know why it took so long for me to get this hype, I think I was just really skeptical about it, I didn't know enough to know for sure one way or another, and _I didn't really care if I didn't get rich_, I just didn't want to be risky. But now, now that it's proven, now that pandora's box has been opened, now that I know it's more than just getting rich, there's really no going back. But, people are still needed to help it's growth, to work on it, develop it, verify transactions, and ensure the network consensus rules are forever adhered to. There is a bit of a religious feeling, like the core beliefs of the followers must be passed down from generation to generation, and it's my job as a libertarian to keep that vision alive.
