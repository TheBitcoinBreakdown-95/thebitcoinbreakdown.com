so the first of the golden rules is Not your keys, not your bitcoin.
	- this is something that was really popularized in the 2015 to 20 22 era I think and has since fallen off quite a bit with the popularity of the ETF's and financial products
	- for those that don't know in 2014 the centralized exchange MT GOX was hacked and 4% of all bitcoin was stolen
	- It was a massive moment in the history of Bitcoin. Many people didn't think it would survive afterwards. The price went from over $1,200 to almost $100.
		- ever since then it was part of Bitcoin culture that you don't trust centralized custodians with your money,
		- in fact removing trusts from financial institutions was the whole point that Bitcoin was even created
		- And since then, we've experienced many other exchange hacks and collapses like Celsius, BlockFi, FTX, and more.
	- so it is important to emphasize that trusted third parties are security holes and everyone should get their bitcoin off of exchanges 


the second golden rule which I truly believe is incredibly important for anyone searching for truth is to not trust, verify
You should not be taking things at face value. Do not trust what you read without rationalizing it for yourself and always look for counter arguments. Read all the sources and then weigh each as unbiased as you can.

By being skeptical, it means you are a rational thinker and don’t just blindly accept things as they are. Which is an integral part of bitcoin, and a foundational value of all bitcoiners.
	- again Bitcoin is all about removing trust it is a trustless monetary system and peer-to-peer value transfer invention and as such one of the core golden rules is to be just as vigilant when learning about Bitcoin and trusting anyone for that matter
	- it is best practice to assume that everyone is a scammer trying to take your money you should do your own research and don't just blindly believe anything that you hear, that includes anything that I say because most people don't know anything.
	- as a part of this golden rule we bitcoiners believe and stand by the core belief that trustless and trust minimize protocols are superior to human social structures

golden rule number 3 is start small. While we believe very strongly and put all of our money on the line of Bitcoin being the superior store of value, it must be emphasized that without conviction newcomers will likely sell at the wrong time or mess up there Bitcoin custody, allocation to Bitcoin increases with knowledge and learning about Bitcoin and being curious will naturally increase your holdings but going all in in the very beginning is something that isn't aligned with the analytical Bitcoin ethos


Golden rule number 4 is stay humble

Matt Odel constantly reminds everyone to stay humble which I think is a value that all of us aspire to but struggle to maintain, ironically I find myself being the most humble after Bitcoin humbles me in a bear market. It's the bull markets where you really have to check yourself. But what does being humble mean? Well First off it means not bragging to people about how much Bitcoin you have, there's a great saying that goes "Always talk about Bitcoin but never talk about your Bitcoins" and that's because Bitcoin the mission is incredibly important to talk about but treating Bitcoin like a get rich scheme undercuts the message and makes you a target.
	- also because of bitcoins free market nature, being greedy can have serious negative consequences since there is no one to come and save you if you mess up. It is very important not to overextend yourself, or to use debt dangerously, it's far better to dollar cost average and lump sum on dips overtime, using limit orders instead of trying to trade around the volatility
	- I coined the phrase buy the dip not the salsa, meaning by the price dips not the spicy hot run-ups
	- it's highly advised not to use leverage or to leverage yourself too dangerously, Bitcoin is a sound money system not a credit system for a reason, and bitcoiners are highly skeptical of credit in general
	- being greedy also has the connotation that you are trying to acquire more dollars, rather than trying to acquire more Bitcoin, that's why so vital not to fall for shitcoin scams, which are all decentralized in name only and follow the build fast and break things philosophy instead of the slow and steady wins the race bitcoiner philosophy
	- Being humble also means controlling your emotions, and no fomoing into the price movements. remember the price will always come back down, don't try to time the market



The 5th golden rule is stack sats and HODL

While everyone has live events and emergencies that come up, I personally never plan on selling my bitcoin. Bitcoin is money, it should be used for payments, not to sell to diversify, or to buy luxuries. Bitcoin is meant to be used, not an investment to pay capital gains taxes on.
There's a certain self sacrificial nature of bitcoiners, that believe in the decentralized movement to both support bitcoin's price for the good of the many, but to also think long term, and sacrifice today to be better off tomorrow. 
The movement isn't about buying dollars, it's about stacking sats, preserving your wealth, and passing it down to future generations.
