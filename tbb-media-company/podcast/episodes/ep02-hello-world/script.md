# EP02: Hello World

**Format:** Podcast (audio only, personal/conversational delivery)
**Target length:** 5-10 min (~1,000-1,500 words spoken)
**Delivery:** This is the most personal episode you'll ever record. No teaching, no structure, just you talking to one person. Brain-dump energy. Let it breathe.
**Source:** My history with bitcoin.md, Purpose.md, Mission.md, North Star.md

---

## COLD OPEN (20 sec)

So... who the hell is this guy talking about bitcoin?

Fair question. Let me tell you.

---

## INTRO (1 min)

Yo it's GC -- and this is The Bitcoin Breakdown.

Before we get into the meat of everything... the education, the breakdowns, all of it... I figured I owe you a proper introduction. Who am I, what is this, and why does it exist.

Because if you're going to spend your time listening to someone talk about the most important invention of our lifetime... you should probably know where they're coming from.

---

## WHO IS GC (2-3 min)

I'm not a financial advisor. I'm not a developer. I don't have a degree in economics or computer science.

I'm a self-taught bitcoiner who spent hundreds of hours going down the rabbit hole... trying to find the catch... and came out the other side more convinced than ever.

And honestly? For most of my life I wasn't even paying attention.

I first heard about bitcoin back in 2012. A buddy of mine was showing me the Silk Road... this dark web marketplace... and he mentioned this internet money you had to use to buy stuff. I thought it was cool but I didn't think twice about it. Why would I? I was in high school.

Then 2015 rolls around. I'm listening to an economics podcast, Econ Talk... and they do an episode on bitcoin. Price was around $250. I had a couple grand in cash. I thought... what if I just bought four of them? But then my inner risk-averse critic kicked in. I figured I missed the boat. Mt. Gox had just collapsed. I convinced myself it was probably a dying fad.

So I didn't buy.

Go figure.

I had a roommate at Cato University that summer... this Austrian economics anarchist from Liechtenstein... who was obsessed with bitcoin. Had it stored on an offline server. Offered to sell me some that the government couldn't track. I forever dubbed him my anarchist bitcoin dealer. But I still didn't really get it. I thought his ideas were a pipe dream.

2017 I bought my first $100. 2018 another $100. But it was still Dad's gamble to me. Not mine.

2020... I wasn't thinking about bitcoin at all. I was depressed, stuck in quarantine, hated my job, didn't know where my career was going. I was completely lost.

And then 2021 hit. Dad mentioned this CEO, Michael Saylor, who was talking to Elon behind closed doors about putting bitcoin on the balance sheet. And I remember thinking... if Elon gets on board, it's game over. So I started paying attention. I spent my first thousand dollars on bitcoin at $51,865.

And then I made a decision that changed everything. I told myself... I don't really know what I'm putting my money into. So I decided to do a little research.

And it was all downhill from there.

---

## THE RABBIT HOLE (1-2 min)

I fell down the rabbit hole hard. Bitcoin Audible. White papers. Austrian economics. Monetary history. Cryptography. Game theory. Hundreds of hours.

And here's what nobody tells you... knowing about bitcoin and understanding bitcoin are two completely different things. I knew about it for almost a decade. But I didn't know anything.

I lack confidence in most of my beliefs. I constantly question everything. I'm usually afraid to share my opinions because I realize everything is nuanced. But bitcoin... bitcoin is the first time I've ever been sure about something. And it took me hundreds of hours to get there.

All the politics I studied, all the economics, the philosophy, the technology... all of my viewpoints and most passionate values... they all connected to this one invention. Like everything in my life had been pointing here and I just didn't see it.

It was almost like destiny.

---

## WHY THIS EXISTS (1-2 min)

So why The Bitcoin Breakdown?

Because the jargon pisses me off. Because it took me way too long to understand something that should be simple. Because there's a ton of misinformation and scammy shit out there, and there need to be more honest voices in the space.

My purpose is simple. I want to help people with bitcoin.

Not sell you on it. Not convince you to buy it. Just... help you understand it. Break it down so you can learn in a fraction of the time it took me.

There's a metaphor I keep coming back to. I'm the one putting people in lifeboats. The boots on the ground, physically helping people onto the new financial system.

That might sound dramatic. But I believe this is the most important financial technology ever created. And the people who need it most... the people getting crushed by inflation, shut out of the banking system, surveilled by their own governments... they're the ones who hear about it last.

Bitcoin won't win by default. It needs educators. It needs storytellers. It needs people who can make the complicated simple and make the simple feel urgent.

That's what this is.

---

## CLOSE (30 sec - 1 min)

So that's me. That's the show. The Bitcoin Breakdown is going to be a place where we go deep on bitcoin... the technology, the history, the philosophy, the culture, all of it... and we do it in a way that's accessible, honest, and hopefully a little entertaining.

I'm not up here because I've got it all figured out. I'm up here because I went looking for the truth, I found it, and now I can't shut up about it.

I'm GC. This is The Bitcoin Breakdown.

Let's go.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Tone shifts to hit:**
- Cold open: Playful, slightly self-deprecating. You're acknowledging the weirdness of talking into a mic.
- Who is GC: Storytelling mode. Let the timeline unfold naturally. The 2012-2015 section should feel like "looking back at a missed opportunity with humor, not regret." The 2020-2021 section gets heavier... depression, being lost, then finding something that gave you purpose.
- The Rabbit Hole: This is the emotional core. "The first time I've ever been sure about something" needs to land. Slow down.
- Why This Exists: Rising conviction. This is where the casual storyteller becomes the educator with a mission. "Lifeboats" is the image you want them to remember.
- Close: Quick, punchy, forward momentum. Don't linger.

**Signature phrases to work in naturally:**
- "Go figure." (after the missed $250 buy)
- "rabbit hole"
- "freedom technology" (if it fits the mission section)

**Rhythm notes:**
- This episode is shorter than the others. Don't pad it. 5-8 minutes is fine.
- The personal sections need room to breathe. Silence after "I was completely lost." Silence after "the first time I've ever been sure about something."
- Don't try to teach anything in this episode. This is all ethos and pathos. The logos comes later.

**Things to NOT do:**
- Don't give a resume or credentials list. You're not credentialed. That's the point.
- Don't talk about price or how much bitcoin you own
- Don't preview every future episode. Just set the vibe.
- Don't over-explain what a podcast is or how to subscribe. Respect the listener.
- Don't sound rehearsed. This should feel like a conversation, not a presentation.
