## 5. Content Strategy

### Production Flow (validated through planning)
1. **Pick** (5-10 min) -- choose ONE topic from existing library
2. **Rant** (10-20 min) -- raw brain dump on the topic, unstructured, transcribe with Whisper
3. **Script** (30-60 min, AI-assisted) -- AI combines best lines from rant + source library research into speakable outline
4. **Record** (20-40 min) -- one take, OBS, no editing pressure
5. **Post-produce** (15-20 min, AI-assisted) -- transcribe, show notes, social, newsletter
6. **Publish** (15 min) -- upload, schedule, distribute

**Total: 2-3 hours per episode.** The rant step adds 10-20 min but produces a better, more authentic script and doubles as speaking practice. Fits alongside full-time job.

### Style & Voice
- Brain-dumping (speech-to-text) > scripting -- feels more natural
- Don't over-script; eyes looking at paper looks bad on camera
- Speech-to-text -> edit -> record approach works well
- Different drawings/slides for each concept help pacing
- Signature opening: "Yo it's GC -- back with another bitcoin breakdown"
- Oratory delivery: dense, pre-written, delivered with conviction
- Mix of reading curated content + personal riffs

## 7. Recurring Patterns to Watch

These themes appear across multiple years of planning documents:

1. **Over-planning vs. shipping.** Every year has detailed quarterly breakdowns. Most years end with less output than planned. The Golden Rules script has been "near-complete" for a while. The antidote is not more planning -- it's recording Episode 1.

2. **Scope creep into systems.** Tendency to build workflows, curricula, automation, and organizational systems before establishing basic output cadence. Manual first, automate after 5-10 episodes.

3. **Self-doubt despite deep knowledge.** "I lack confidence in my beliefs and constantly question everything" appears alongside evidence of years of research and community teaching. The knowledge base is solid -- the gap is reps, not preparation.

4. **Perfectionism as a blocker.** Multiple mentions of wanting everything polished before publishing. Brain-dumping and one-take recording are the proven antidotes (per the user's own notes).

5. **Balance tension.** Real tension between indoor learning/creating and outdoor living/dating/friends. The solution documented in Goals Refined: 5 nights/week for personal work (1hr each), balance with relationship/social/family time.

## 8. Teaching Pedagogy (Summary)

Full framework in `business/teaching-framework.md`. Key principles:

- **Constructivist-cognitivist hybrid:** Use stories + visuals (cognitivist inputs) to help learners build understanding through application (constructivist output)
- **Inspiration-first:** Open with a burning question or emotional hook before teaching content
- **Absorption tactics:** Spaced repetition, retrieval practice, dual coding (words + visuals), concrete examples, emotional engagement
- **Inquiry before answers:** Ask the question, let it sit, then teach
- **Peer teaching as highest learning form:** Design content that makes the viewer want to explain it to someone else
